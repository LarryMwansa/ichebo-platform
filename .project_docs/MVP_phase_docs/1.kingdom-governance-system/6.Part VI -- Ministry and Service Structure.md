
# **Part V – Ministry and Service Structure**

## **19. Kingdom Participation Model**

The Kingdom Participation Model defines how individuals engage, grow, and function within the Kingdom Governance System. It establishes a **clear pathway from belonging to responsibility**, ensuring that every individual is both **formed and deployed** within the life of the Kingdom.

### **19.1 Purpose of the Participation Model**

The model exists to:

- provide a **structured entry point** into Kingdom life
    
- guide individuals from **discipleship to service and leadership**
    
- ensure that participation is **intentional, not passive**
    
- align every individual with a **function within the system**
    

### **19.2 Levels of Participation**

Participation within the Kingdom Governance System unfolds progressively across five levels:

#### **1. Connection**

Individuals are introduced to the community and begin engaging with Kingdom life.

#### **2. Formation**

Individuals enter structured discipleship and spiritual development through the Formation Layer.

#### **3. Alignment**

Individuals are aligned with Kingdom values, governance culture, and community life.

#### **4. Service**

Individuals are activated into specific service roles within defined Service Domains.

#### **5. Leadership**

Individuals are developed and entrusted with leadership responsibilities based on competence and calling.

---

### **19.3 Participation Principles**

The Kingdom Participation Model is governed by the following principles:

- **Every believer is called to participate**
    
- Participation must lead to **formation and transformation**
    
- Service flows from **identity and calling**, not obligation
    
- Growth is both **spiritual and functional**
    
- Leadership emerges from **faithfulness in service**
    

---

### **19.4 Integration with Governance System**

The Participation Model is directly connected to:

- the **Eight Kingdom Pathways** (formation journey)
    
- the **Roles & Competence Framework** (readiness and capability)
    
- the **Administrative Offices** (oversight and coordination)
    

This ensures that participation is not isolated, but fully **integrated into governance, formation, and mission**.

---

## **20. Service Domains**

Service Domains define the **primary areas of Kingdom activity** through which ministry is expressed and operations are carried out.

They provide structure for organizing service while allowing for **flexibility and contextual expression** across communities and networks.

### **20.1 Purpose of Service Domains**

Service Domains exist to:

- organize Kingdom service into **clear functional areas**
    
- ensure **balanced ministry expression**
    
- align service with **governance structures**
    
- provide clarity for **placement and development of individuals**
    

---

### **20.2 Core Service Domains**

All Kingdom service is organized into six core domains:

#### **1. Apostolic & Spiritual Ministry**

Focuses on spiritual oversight, prayer, teaching, and doctrinal alignment.

#### **2. Leadership & Governance Support**

Supports leadership structures, coordination, and governance processes.

#### **3. Formation & Teaching**

Responsible for discipleship, training, and spiritual development.

#### **4. Mission & Outreach**

Focused on evangelism, outreach, and expansion initiatives.

#### **5. Community Life & Care**

Builds and sustains healthy, relational, and supportive communities.

#### **6. Operations & Stewardship**

Handles administration, logistics, finance, and resource management.

---

### **20.3 Domain Alignment**

Each Service Domain aligns with:

- one or more of the **Seven Governance Pillars**
    
- relevant **Administrative Offices**
    
- specific **Kingdom Pathways**
    

This ensures that all service is:

- **governed**
    
- **coordinated**
    
- **measurable**
    

---

## **21. The Twenty-Four Orders of Kingdom Service**

The Twenty-Four Orders of Kingdom Service represent the **formal structure of service roles** within the Kingdom Governance System.

Each Order is a **recognized stream of service**, through which individuals contribute to the life, growth, and mission of the Kingdom.

---

## **21.1 Purpose of the Orders**

The Orders exist to:

- provide **clear and structured service roles**
    
- ensure **balanced distribution of responsibilities**
    
- support **leadership development pathways**
    
- enable **scalable participation across communities and networks**
    

---

## **21.2 Structure of the Orders**

The Twenty-Four Orders are distributed across the six Service Domains, with **four Orders per domain**, ensuring balance and completeness.

---

### **A. Apostolic & Spiritual Ministry**

1. **Order of Apostolic Service**  
    (Oversight, alignment, and governance support)
    
2. **Order of Prophetic Ministry**  
    (Discernment, intercession, and spiritual direction)
    
3. **Order of Teaching and Doctrine**  
    (Biblical instruction and doctrinal formation)
    
4. **Order of Prayer and Intercession**  
    (Sustaining spiritual covering and prayer movements)
    

---

### **B. Leadership & Governance Support**

5. **Order of Governance Support**  
    (Policy support, coordination, and compliance)
    
6. **Order of Strategic Coordination**  
    (Planning, alignment, and execution support)
    
7. **Order of Leadership Assistance**  
    (Direct support to leaders and offices)
    
8. **Order of Communication and Alignment**  
    (Information flow and organizational unity)
    

---

### **C. Formation & Teaching**

9. **Order of Discipleship Facilitation**  
    (Guiding individuals through formation pathways)
    
10. **Order of Training and Instruction**  
    (Delivering structured learning programmes)
    
11. **Order of Mentorship and Coaching**  
    (Developing individuals and leaders)
    
12. **Order of Curriculum Development**  
    (Designing learning materials and systems)
    

---

### **D. Mission & Outreach**

13. **Order of Evangelism**  
    (Proclaiming the message of the Kingdom)
    
14. **Order of Mission Teams**  
    (Coordinated outreach and mission work)
    
15. **Order of Community Outreach**  
    (Local engagement and impact initiatives)
    
16. **Order of Expansion and Planting**  
    (Establishing new communities and works)
    

---

### **E. Community Life & Care**

17. **Order of Pastoral Care**  
    (Providing care, guidance, and support)
    
18. **Order of Community Building**  
    (Strengthening relationships and unity)
    
19. **Order of Hospitality and Welcome**  
    (Creating welcoming environments)
    
20. **Order of Welfare and Support**  
    (Meeting practical and social needs)
    

---

### **F. Operations & Stewardship**

21. **Order of Administration**  
    (Operational coordination and systems)
    
22. **Order of Resource Management**  
    (Handling finances and materials)
    
23. **Order of Logistics and Events**  
    (Coordinating events and programmes)
    
24. **Order of Media and Communication**  
    (Content, media, and communication systems)
    

---

## **21.3 Participation and Progression within Orders**

Individuals may:

- begin service within an Order based on **availability and guidance**
    
- grow in responsibility through **formation and competence development**
    
- transition between Orders as **calling and capability develop**
    
- be recognised and affirmed based on **faithfulness and fruitfulness**
    

---

## **21.4 Governance of the Orders**

The Orders are:

- overseen by relevant **Administrative Offices**
    
- aligned with **Service Domains**
    
- integrated into the **Calendars & Programmes Framework**
    

Each Order operates under:

- defined leadership
    
- clear responsibilities
    
- measurable outcomes
    

---

## **21.5 Function within the Kingdom Governance System**

The Twenty-Four Orders serve as the **primary mechanism for mobilising the Body into action**.

They ensure that:

- every individual has a **place to serve**
    
- every function within the system is **covered**
    
- leadership development is **continuous and practical**
    
- the Kingdom Mandate is expressed through **coordinated action**

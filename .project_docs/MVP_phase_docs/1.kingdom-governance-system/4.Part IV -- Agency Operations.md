
# 4.1.1 The Apostolic Directive

The Apostolic Directive is the mechanism through which the Kingdom Mandate becomes:

- Specific
    
- Assigned
    
- Actionable
    

It translates divine intent into:

- Defined missions
    
- Targeted fields
    
- Measurable outcomes
    

---

## 4.1.2 Nature of the Directive

Unlike the global mandate, the Apostolic Directive is:

- Contextual
    
- Assigned to individuals or groups
    
- Focused on specific outcomes
    

It answers:

- Who is sent
    
- Where they are sent
    
- What they are sent to accomplish
    

---

## 4.1.3 Formation of Agency

From the Apostolic Directive emerges **Agency**.

Agency is the operational expression of governance — it is where:

- People are mobilised
    
- Systems are built
    
- Work is carried out
    

Agency includes:

- Envoys (sent ones)
    
- Operators (functional executors)
    
- Systems (structures of work)
    

---

## 4.1.4 Dual Structure of Operation

Agency operates through two essential dimensions:

### Upward Service

- Alignment with God
    
- Prayer
    
- Word
    
- Spiritual oversight
    

### Downward Service

- Administration
    
- Distribution
    
- Execution
    
- System management
    

Both are essential and must remain in balance.

---

## 4.1.5 The Acts 6 Protocol (Operational Foundation)

Acts 6 provides the foundational model for sustainable Kingdom operation.

It establishes:

- Separation of focus
    
- Delegation of responsibility
    
- Preservation of priority
    

The apostles maintained:

- Prayer
    
- Ministry of the Word
    

While others were appointed for:

- Daily administration
    
- Distribution and operational tasks
    

---

## 4.1.6 Principles from Acts 6

From this model, the following principles emerge:

### 1. Delegation is essential

### 2. Priorities must be preserved

### 3. Structure prevents breakdown

### 4. Growth requires systemisation

---

## 4.1.7 Operational Outcome

When Agency functions correctly:

- The mandate advances
    
- Governance remains intact
    
- Systems scale effectively

# 4.2 Agency Operations Framework

## 4.2.1 The Agency 

The Agency functions as the governing instrument responsible for stewarding the Kingdom mandate and coordinating the mission of the movement.

In order to facilitate the **Kingdom Mandate** and its operational framework, we propose the formation of **The Agency**, which acts as the Kingdom of God’s delegation tasked with developing and implementing this mandate.

The Agency exists to coordinate **personnel, resources, and operations** necessary for advancing the work of the Kingdom. It serves as the organisational instrument through which the Lord’s mandate is translated into structured action within communities, institutions, and society.

At its core, the Agency is designed to function according to what we refer to as the **Acts 6 Protocol** (Acts 6:1–7), which provides a model for balancing spiritual oversight with operational service. In this model, leadership ensures that the mission of Christ advances effectively by organizing people, structures, and responsibilities in a way that preserves both spiritual direction and practical service.

Through this protocol, the Agency operates along two complementary directions of service:

### 4.2.1.1 Service Towards the Master

This dimension performs the **prophetic and apostolic functions** of the movement. Its responsibility is to discern the strategic objectives of the Lord, provide spiritual direction, and ensure that the work of the movement remains aligned with the will and purposes of Christ.

### 4.2.1.2 Service Towards the People

This dimension performs the **implementation and mobilization functions** of the movement. It focuses on organising believers, communities, and operational structures that carry out the mandate in everyday life and in the wider society.

Through the interaction of these two dimensions, the Agency seeks to faithfully advance the work of Christ while effectively serving the communities in which the Kingdom mandate is being expressed.

---

## 4.2.2 Core Institutional Components

The work of the Agency is organised through several **Core Institutional Components**. These components serve as the management instruments through which the mission of the Agency is coordinated and implemented.

## 4.2.3 Executive Team

The **Executive Team** serves as the general leadership body responsible for carrying out the Lord’s mandate through the Agency.

The Executive Team provides both **spiritual and administrative leadership**, ensuring that the work of the Agency remains aligned with the Kingdom vision while also maintaining effective organisational governance. It coordinates the strategic direction of the movement and ensures that the various branches of the Agency operate in unity toward the fulfilment of the mandate.

---

## 4.2.4 Disciple Ministry Network (DMN)

The **Disciple Ministry Network (DMN)** forms the relational and operational network of the Agency’s delegates. It brings together individuals and ministries that serve as active participants in advancing the Kingdom mandate.

Through the DMN, believers are connected into a collaborative structure where they can contribute their gifts, ministries, and callings toward the larger mission.

The DMN consists of several specialised branches that facilitate different aspects of Kingdom service.

### 4.2.4.1 Allied Prophetic and Apostolic Services (APAS)

The **Allied Prophetic and Apostolic Services (APAS)** branch is responsible for the spiritual direction and establishment of the ministry according to the guidance of the Lord.

This branch provides apostolic and prophetic oversight to the movement, ensuring that the mission remains aligned with the purposes and leading of Christ. It facilitates discernment, spiritual governance, and strategic direction for the Agency.

This branch operates according to the **APAS Framework**, which governs how prophetic and apostolic functions contribute to the guidance of the movement.

---

### 4.2.4.2 Special Envoys Networking Division (SEND)

The **Special Envoys Networking Division (SEND)** is responsible for mobilising and connecting believers who serve as delegates of the Agency.

SEND functions both as a **leadership network and a missionary deployment network**, organising individuals who are committed to serving within the Disciple Ministry Network and contributing to the broader mission of the Kingdom.

Through SEND, believers are equipped, mobilised, and deployed into areas of service where their gifts and calling can be effectively expressed.

This branch operates according to the **SENDMe Framework**, which governs the mobilisation, coordination, and deployment of Kingdom delegates.

---

## 4.2.5 Network of Communities (NC)

The **Network of Communities (NC)** represents the decentralised structure through which the Kingdom mandate is implemented at the grassroots level.

It serves as the operational interface between the Agency and the wider society, enabling communities of believers to engage their local environments with the principles and service of the Kingdom.

The Network of Communities is designed specifically to overcome the problem of **institutional silos**, allowing believers to work collaboratively across churches, ministries, and communities in advancing the mission of Christ.

Through this structure, Kingdom mobilisation becomes **distributed, localised, and community-driven**.

---

##  4.2.5.1 The Structure of Sceptre Communities

Within the Network of Communities, the primary operational unit is the **Sceptre Community**.

Sceptre Communities function as **local Church Nodes**, similar to small groups or home-based church cells. They represent the local expression of the Kingdom within a particular community or geographic area.

The aim is to restore the presence of Kingdom community life at the most local level possible, allowing each neighbourhood or district to have a living representation of the Kingdom of God.

Each Sceptre Community is organised by members of that collective and coordinated by a **Community Coordinator**, who acts as the representative of that community within the broader network.

---

##  4.2.5.2 Church Collectives

When **three or more Sceptre Communities** exist within a given geographic demarcation, they may form a **Church Collective**.

A Church Collective represents a cooperative network of Church Nodes operating within a shared area of responsibility, such as wards within a district or districts within a region.

In order to qualify as a Church Collective, the participating communities must demonstrate evidence of active Kingdom work through their contributions to society and the advancement of the mission.

Church Collectives play an important role in the growth of the Network of Communities by supporting new communities and helping establish Kingdom presence in previously uncharted areas.

This organic model of expansion allows the Kingdom network to grow **through multiplication rather than centralisation**.

---

##  4.2.5.3 Community as a Collective Person

A central principle within the Network of Communities is the understanding that a community may function as a **Collective Person**.

Just as an individual possesses gifts, purpose, and responsibilities before God, a constituted Kingdom community likewise carries collective gifts, calling, and responsibilities.

Within this framework:

- Individuals serve God through their personal giftings and callings.
    
- Families participate in the life of the community.
    
- Communities together express the collective gifts and purposes entrusted to them by God.
    

Through this understanding, communities are encouraged to discover and express their unique contributions to the work of the Kingdom.

---

## 4.2.5.4 The Work of a Sceptre Community

The work of a Sceptre Community within its **Area of Operation (A.O.)** is guided by the three interconnected dimensions of Kingdom influence.

Each community functions with the responsibility to:

1. Serve the Collective Person.
    
2. Contribute the gifts entrusted to the Collective Person.
    
3. Remain receptive to the grace and transformation that flows from Christ.
    

---

## 4.2.5.5 Duties of the Sceptre Community

Each community possesses a combination of **spiritual gifts, natural abilities, and vocational skills** that determine how it serves its area of operation.

The visible work of a community demonstrates the heart of that collective and forms the basis for engagement with the wider society.

For example, a community whose members possess strong educational gifts may choose to support local learners through tutoring and educational initiatives. Through such service, the community creates opportunities to demonstrate the love of Christ and introduce Kingdom principles within society.

These acts of service become both **a testimony of faith and a means of building peace within the community**.

---

## 4.2.5.6 Contribution Through Giftings

Within the Sceptre Community, each member is encouraged to operate according to the gifts and calling entrusted to them by God.

As individuals grow in their personal transformation in Christ, they are able to guide and support others while contributing meaningfully to the life of the community.

This collaborative expression of giftings strengthens the overall mission of the community and enables it to function effectively within its area of operation.

---

## 4.2.5.7 Receptivity to the Grace of Service

Members of the community are also called to remain receptive to the **grace that flows from Christ**.

By continually aligning their hearts, thinking, and actions with the character of the Lord, they become instruments through which grace can be extended to others.

Through this process, believers minister simultaneously:

- to the Lord,
    
- to their personal calling,
    
- and to the communities they serve.

# 4.3 The 7-Pillar Governance Architecture


The **Agency** governs the Kingdom mission through seven functional pillars.

These pillars ensure that **vision, doctrine, formation, mission, communities, administration, and resources** all move together.

The Seven Governance pillars are as follows:

1.  Apostolic and Spiritual Oversight
2.  Strategy and Governance
3.  Formation and Discipleship
4.  Programmes and Ministry Operations
5.  Mission and Expansion
6.  Communities and Networks
7.  Stewardship and Administration

## 4.3.1 Pillar 1 — Apostolic & Spiritual Oversight

This pillar protects the **spiritual integrity of the movement**.

It ensures that doctrine, prophetic direction, and apostolic alignment remain intact.

### Functions

- Guard doctrine and theology
    
- Provide apostolic covering
    
- Provide prophetic insight and discernment
    
- Resolve spiritual and leadership disputes
    
- Preserve the identity and calling of the movement
    

### Structures

- Apostolic Council
    
- Prophetic Council
    
- Spiritual Elders
    

### Primary Network

**APAS** (Apostolic–Prophetic–Apostolic Support)

---

- Movement expansion strategy

## 4.3.9 Why Seven Pillars?

Seven is not arbitrary.

Historically, **every enduring apostolic movement** ends up needing these seven domains:

1. Spiritual authority
    
2. Governance
    
3. Formation
    
4. Programmes
    
5. Mission
    
6. Community structures
    
7. Administration
    

Without one of these pillars, movements usually collapse due to:

- doctrinal drift
    
- leadership conflict
    
- lack of trained leaders
    
- poor operations
    
- uncontrolled expansion

**PROPHET\'S HANDBOOK**

*Structure, Record Types & Design Reference*

Integrated Community System · Governance App Series

Preamble

The handbook is not one tool among many --- it is the whole. Every
output of this ministry is the handbook made external. The mandate, the
platform, the communities, the governance system: all are the handbook
translated into different forms.

This document defines the structure of the handbook, the organisation of
its branches, the record types that populate it, and the methodology
that underlies it. It is intended to serve both as a reference for the
founding apostle and as the design specification for the Governance App
within the Integrated Community System (ICS).

Part I --- Apostolic Properties

1.1 Overview

Apostolic Properties is the master name for the entire body of work that
belongs to the apostolic office. It is the parent structure under which
all handbook material is organised. Within it sit three distinct
branches, each with a different source, direction of flow, and function.

*Each branch has a distinct direction of flow:*

-   **The Reference Library** --- flows inward --- you gather and
    compile into it from Scripture

-   **The Keys Library** --- flows inward and outward --- you record
    into it and draw from it to interpret

-   **The Mandate** --- flows outward --- what is received is translated
    into work, platform, and community

**Branch 1 --- The Reference Library**

1.2 The Reference Library

The Reference Library --- also known as the Prophet\'s Library --- is a
compiled collection of Properties drawn from Holy Scripture using the
Hierarchy and Relationship System (HRS). It is the objective library:
the same body of knowledge for every operator, built from the primary
source of divine communication.

The Reference Library holds two categories of material:

-   **Biblical Concepts and Principles** --- the foundational ideas and
    rules that underpin the Word of God, compiled so that members of the
    Kingdom can access any principle without extensive searching

-   **Biblical Symbolism** --- the accumulated symbolic language used in
    Scripture and in divine communication, essential for interpretive
    work

*The Reference Library was previously referred to as both the
\'Reference Library\' and the \'Properties Library.\' These are one and
the same. The content of the library is Properties; the library is the
collection of those Properties.*

1.2.2 Property Attributes

Each Property entry carries descriptive attributes that characterise its
nature and relational position:

-   **Complexity** --- the structural depth of the property

-   **Relationship** --- subjective (ruling) or objective (subservient)

-   **Position** --- up/down, left/right

-   **Direction** --- forward or backward

-   **Speed** --- fast or slow

-   **Emotional Tone** --- e.g. fearful, dread, vengeful

**Branch 2 --- The Keys Library**

1.3 The Keys Library

The Keys Library --- also known as the Dream Library --- is the personal
and individual reference library built by each operator over time. While
the Reference Library is objective and shared, the Keys Library is
subjective and unique to each person.

Its source data is the Dream Journal and the Spirit Journal. As the
operator records dreams and spiritual communications over time,
recurring patterns, characters, and symbols emerge. These are collected,
interpreted, and held as interpretive keys --- a personal framework for
deciphering the messages received in ongoing divine communication.

-   **Source** --- Dream Journal and Spirit Journal (input records)

-   **Output** --- Personal Keys --- individual symbol-to-meaning
    mappings

-   **Duration** --- Initial key development requires 2--3 months of
    consistent dream recording to reveal recurring patterns

**Branch 3 --- The Mandate**

1.4 The Mandate

The Mandate branch holds all material received, decreed, and established
through apostolic function. Unlike the two libraries --- which compile
and interpret --- the Mandate records what has been given and translates
it into the work of the Kingdom.

The Mandate flows outward: from divine reception, to written record, to
strategic translation, to deployed platform and community.

1.4.1 Mandate Sub-Components

-   **Foundational Mandate** --- the core declaration given by the Lord;
    the originating word from which all else flows

-   **Strategic Directives** --- objectives, instructions, and
    assignments given over time

-   **Governance Documents** --- the translated outputs of the mandate
    --- the KGS, the ICS platform, and all operational frameworks

Part II --- The Hierarchy and Relationship System

2.1 Overview

The Hierarchy and Relationship System (HRS)® is the interpretive
methodology that underlies all handbook work. It is the methodological
backbone governing how divine communication is received, processed,
organised, and understood.

HRS is versatile: it can be applied to biblical text, biblical
symbolism, and dream symbolism. It is the tool that produces Properties
for the Reference Library, and the framework through which the Keys
Library is built and used.

2.2 The Four Steps of HRS

-   **Step 1 --- Determining the Ruling Influence** --- identify which
    of the two governing laws is active in the narration: Selflessness
    (Kingdom of Light) or Self-centredness (Kingdom of Darkness)

-   **Step 2 --- Determining Hierarchy and Relationship** --- sort the
    data as subjects and objects, establishing positional and relational
    interaction

-   **Step 3 --- Compilation of Databases** --- organise all material
    according to the General Signification of Symbols, sourced from
    Scripture

-   **Step 4 --- Categorisation** --- classify all compiled material
    under the four Property types: Classes, Principles, Concepts, Divine
    Patterns

Part III --- Journals

Journals are the input instruments of the handbook. They are the primary
data-gathering tools. Each journal has a specific function and feeds
into specific output records or library branches.

-   **General Journal** --- records thoughts, activities, and daily
    reflections. Feeds the Daily Analysis Record (DAR) and general
    reference records.

-   **Prayer Journal** --- records and tracks prayer campaigns from
    entry to answered. Links to the Activity App and collaborates with
    the Spirit Journal.

-   **Spirit Journal** --- records words and inspirations received from
    the Lord. Primary feeder of the Keys Library and the Mandate branch.

-   **Dream Journal** --- records dreams as received, as vividly as
    possible. Primary feeder of the Keys Library.

Part V --- Handbook as Governance App

5.1 Purpose

The Governance App within ICS is the digital expression of the handbook.
It provides the founding apostle and, progressively, Level 3--5
operators with the tools to build, maintain, and draw from the Apostolic
Properties.

5.2 Dashboard Levels

-   **General Dashboard** --- registered users; basic features

-   **Members Dashboard** --- Level 1 users; designated member features

-   **Operators Dashboard** --- Level 2--5 users; full toolkit access

-   **Handbook Dashboard** --- Level 5 governance operators; full
    Apostolic Properties access

5.3 Training Progression

The handbook is the body of work through which Level 3, 4, and 5
operators are trained in mandate creation and systems creation. By Level
5, operators are equipped to evaluate whether they hold to this handbook
or, in time, develop their own --- having mastered the methodology that
produced it.

*The whole entire work is the handbook. What goes into the handbook is
translated out into everything.*

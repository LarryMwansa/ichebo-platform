The Sceptre Community Programme

You don't need
a building.
You need a calling.

Three people meeting in a home. A family committed to something more. A group of colleagues who sense they could do more together than apart. That is enough to begin.

The Sceptre Community Programme recognises the smallest gathering as a legitimate unit of Kingdom operation — and gives it everything it needs to grow from there.

What It Is

From the ground up.
Not the top down.

The Sceptre Community Programme begins with the smallest recognised unit of Kingdom life — and builds outward from there, through neighbourhoods, districts, provinces, and nations.

It is not a denomination. Not a course you attend and leave. It is a way of being a community — with shared values, clear structure, and a path for every person inside it to grow and contribute.

Each Sceptre Community operates as a Church Node — a living point in a wider network of communities all aligned to the same purpose: to see Kingdom life take root in the neighbourhoods, streets, and workplaces around them.

Structure
Clear roles. Shared responsibility.

Defined governance so that no one person carries everything alone. Authority is shared, decisions are clear, and the community can function even when its leader is absent.

Formation
A path for every person.

From the moment someone joins, there is a clear journey — through stages of growth that develop real capability, not just knowledge. People become stewards, not just attendees.

Network
Connected to others doing the same work.

No gathering operates as an island. Church Nodes connect across the movement — sharing resources, accountability, and the collective capacity that no single community could produce alone.

Platform
The tool that makes it manageable.

The Ichebo Platform handles governance records, formation tracking, and network connection — so communities can focus on people, not paperwork.

Community Life

A community has
three responsibilities.

A Sceptre Community is not just a gathering of people. It takes on the character of a person before God — with gifts, calling, and responsibility in three directions.

01 — Upward
Responsibility to God

The community's life of worship, prayer, and alignment with Christ. The source from which everything else flows — and the measure by which all other activity is kept honest.

02 — Inward
Responsibility to the Family

The community's care for the households and people within it. The home is the first proving ground of Kingdom life — and a community that neglects its own people cannot reach those outside.

03 — Outward
Responsibility to Society

The community's visible presence and service in the neighbourhood around it. Getting involved. Seeing people. Taking care of each other — and then the street outside the door.

Start a Community

How it begins.

Starting a Sceptre Community requires three committed people and a sense of calling. Not a building. Not a denomination. Not a large group. Here is the process.

1
Gather your people
Identify two or more others who share your sense of calling. Three people with genuine commitment to Kingdom community life is enough to begin. No building required — in-person, digital, or hybrid are all recognised.
2
Understand your community's shape
The Triple-Thread Survey helps you understand your community's spiritual gifts, natural abilities, and vocational skills — and shapes the distinctive character of your contribution to your area.
3
Define where you serve
Identify the geographic or vocational territory where your community will operate. Your neighbourhood. Your workplace. The street where people live who have no one paying attention to them.
4
Register with the network
Connect with Ichebo Christian Services to register your community, receive your governance orientation, and join the Ichebo Platform. You become a Church Node — part of something larger than your gathering.
5
Begin the journey
Your Community Coordinator and members begin their formation pathway. Each person has a clear path. The community begins to operate — not just meet.
What You Need to Begin
3 or more committed peopleNo minimum congregation size beyond this.
A Community CoordinatorThe representative and steward of the community within the network.
A defined area to serveA neighbourhood, workplace, or community you are committed to.
Commitment to the three responsibilitiesTo God, to the family within, and to the society around you.

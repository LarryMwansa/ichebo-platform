The Foundation

The dominant institutional form of the modern church produces strong institutions but weak networks. The Sceptre Community Programme is built on a different principle: begin with the smallest legitimate expression of the church and build upward.

Rather than distributing activity downward from an institution, the programme returns church to the community. It identifies and establishes Church Nodes — small, constituted gatherings of believers — and networks them upward through the civic geography of every nation, from neighbourhoods to global collectives.

A Sceptre Community may be as small as three or four people. A family. A household fellowship. A vocational group. A neighbourhood prayer circle. What matters is not size or configuration — it is the legitimacy of function: the ability to pray, disciple, govern itself, serve its community, and be accountable within the broader movement.

The Church Node

The Sceptre Community functions as a Church Node — the smallest recognised unit of church life, governance, discipleship, and Kingdom operation within the network.

The Collective Person

When believers constitute a Sceptre Community, that community takes on person status. It carries collective gifts, collective calling, and collective responsibility before God — just as an individual does.

Territorial Governance

Communities are organised across the civic geography of the land — branch, district, province, nation, region, continent — mirroring the natural structures of society rather than imposing an ecclesiastical hierarchy upon it.

The Five-Level Architecture
L1
Kingdom Mandate
The divine authority layer — Christ's commission to His Church. The governing mission and theological foundation of the entire system.
L2
Apostolic Governance — The Agency
The governing authority responsible for stewarding the mandate. Ichebo Christian Services occupies this position — the coordinating instrument for the movement.
L3
Networks & Church Collectives
Regional or thematic groupings of churches and ministries working collaboratively. Three or more Sceptre Communities within a geographic demarcation form a Church Collective.
L4
Sceptre Communities — Church Nodes
The foundational unit. Local disciple-making communities representing the rule of Christ in a locality. The smallest legitimate expression of the church.
L5
Individual Disciples
Kingdom citizens. The entire structure exists to form, equip, and deploy individuals through discipleship, competence development, and ministry deployment.

A movement pipeline from mandate to disciple.

This five-level structure reflects the pattern of the New Testament — from Christ's commission to the apostles, through apostolic leadership teams and mission networks, to churches in cities and house churches in neighbourhoods. It is not an imported management theory. It is the apostolic pattern recovered for the present age.

Why "Sceptre"?

A sceptre symbolises sovereignty, authority, power, and the right to rule. Scripture declares that righteousness is the sceptre of God's Kingdom (Psalm 45:6; Hebrews 1:8). The name is not decorative — it declares that the rule of Christ is exercised through righteousness practised in His people, not through force or domination.

Start a Community

You don't need a building.
You need a calling.

Starting a Sceptre Community begins with three or more people committed to functioning as a Church Node. Here is the process.

1
Gather Your People
Identify two or more others who share your sense of calling. You don't need a building, a denomination, or a large group. Three people with a genuine commitment to Kingdom community life is enough to begin.
2
Complete the Triple-Thread Survey
The Triple-Thread Survey helps you understand your community's spiritual gifts, natural abilities, and vocational skills. This shapes your Community Theme — the distinctive character of your contribution to your Area of Operation.
3
Define Your Area of Operation
Identify the geographic or vocational territory where your community will serve. This determines your position within the civic-geographic network and your responsibilities to society.
4
Register with the Network
Connect with Ichebo Christian Services to register your Sceptre Community, receive your governance orientation, and join the Ichebo Platform. You become a Church Node in the broader movement.
5
Begin Formation
Enrol your Community Coordinator and members in the formation pathway. Discipleship, service, and qualification programmes are accessible through the Ichebo Platform and through your network.
